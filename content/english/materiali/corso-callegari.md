**Emanuele Callegari** is a professor of Mathematical Analysis at the **Department of Mathematics of the University of Rome “Tor Vergata”**, where he is also involved in activities related to the Mathematics Olympiads.

Since **2006**, he has been a member of the **Olympiad Commission of the Italian Mathematical Union (UMI)**.

When he was a high school student, he achieved excellent results in mathematics competitions:

- **first and fourth place** at the national finals of the Mathematics Olympiad  
- member of the Italian team at the **International Mathematical Olympiad (IMO)** in **1985** and **1986**

His preferred olympiad field is **combinatorics**.

---

One of the best starting points for students who want to begin studying olympiad mathematics is his course.

The course is available here: 🎬 https://www.problemisvolti.it/CorsoBaseOlimpiadiMatematica.html

It is a series of **introductory video lectures** designed for students approaching the Mathematics Olympiads for the first time.

The recommendation is to follow the lessons **in order, from the first to the last**, in order to gradually build a solid theoretical foundation.

---

### Materials from Olympiad Training Camps

In addition to the introductory course, Prof. Callegari has also made available several **teaching materials** used during olympiad training camps.

These materials are available here: 📄 https://www.problemisvolti.it/StageOlimpiadiMatematica.html

They consist of lecture notes and materials used in training camps for the Mathematics Olympiads, which are very useful for studying several topics in greater depth.
